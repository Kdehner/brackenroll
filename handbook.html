Rules Reference

Player Handbook

Everything You Need to Play

Daggerheart runs on a simple loop: your character tries something uncertain, you roll to find out what happens. The twist is that every roll produces two things at once — an outcome for you, and an outcome for the world.

The Six Traits

Every action roll uses one of your six traits. Your GM will tell you which one, or it'll be obvious from what you're doing. Your trait score is a modifier you add to your roll.

Agility

Speed, reflexes, and movement. Rolling to sprint, dodge, leap, or sneak.

Strength

Raw physical force. Rolling to lift, grapple, break, or overpower something.

Finesse

Precision and dexterity. Rolling for ranged attacks, lockpicking, sleight of hand.

Instinct

Awareness and intuition. Rolling to perceive danger, track, or read a situation.

Presence

Charisma and force of will. Rolling to persuade, intimidate, deceive, or perform.

Knowledge

Learning and recall. Rolling to identify magic, remember lore, or apply expertise.

The Duality Dice

Making a Roll

When you attempt something with an uncertain outcome, roll two twelve-sided dice — a Hope Die and a Fear Die — and add a modifier from one of your six traits.

Add both dice and your modifier together. Compare that total to the Difficulty set by your GM. Succeed or fail, the comparison between the two dice determines whether you or the GM gains something.

Hope

Hope is your resource — earned when your Hope Die rolls higher than your Fear Die. You can hold up to 6 Hope at a time, and it carries between sessions.

Spend Hope to:

  • Give an ally a d6 advantage die (1 Hope)
  • Use an Experience (1 Hope)
  • Initiate a Tag Team Roll with an ally (3 Hope)
  • Activate your class's Hope Feature (usually 3 Hope)

Fear

When your Fear Die rolls higher than your Hope Die, the GM gains a Fear token — even if you succeeded. Fear isn't failure; it's the world pushing back.

The GM spends Fear to spotlight adversaries, trigger complications, introduce new threats, or activate danger moves. A session with a lot of Fear is a tense one.

Stress

Stress is mental and physical strain. You have up to 5 Stress slots. Each marked Stress reduces your maximum Hope by 1 — so a stressed-out character can't hold as much good fortune.

Mark a Stress to: add a d6 advantage die to any action roll, or activate certain features. Stress clears on a critical success or during downtime.

What Your Roll Means

Critical
Both dice show the same number — automatic success, gain 1 Hope, and clear 1 Stress, even if your total was below Difficulty.
Success with Hope
Total meets Difficulty and Hope Die is higher — you succeed and gain 1 Hope.
Success with Fear
Total meets Difficulty but Fear Die is higher — you succeed, but the GM gains 1 Fear. Something complicates the moment.
Failure with Hope
Total falls short but Hope Die is higher — you fail, but gain 1 Hope. The door didn't open, but you found something useful.
Failure with Fear
Total falls short and Fear Die is higher — you fail and the GM gains 1 Fear. Never the end of the story, but never easy either.

Domain Cards & Your Loadout

  • You can have at most 5 domain cards active at a time. Once you have 6 or more, extras go into your vault.
  • When leveling up, you can swap one vaulted card for one in your loadout — or trade a current card for a different one of equal or lower level.
  • Domain cards from a shared domain: if you and another player both use the same domain, coordinate so you're not stepping on each other's choices.
  • When multiclassing, domain cards from your new class are limited to half your current level (rounded up).

The whole party levels up together when your GM calls a milestone. Most groups play at least three sessions between levels. Characters go from level 1 to 10 across four tiers of play.

Tiers of Play

Tier 1

Level 1

Starting characters. Establishing your identity, early abilities, and how you fit into the world.

Tier 2

Levels 2–4

Early growth. Proficiency increases, new domain cards, building toward your subclass specialization.

Tier 3

Levels 5–7

Mid-tier power. Subclass upgrades, increased Proficiency, and the option to multiclass.

Tier 4

Levels 8–10

High-tier play. Mastery features, unrestricted domain access, peak character potential.

When You Level Up

Step 1 — Take Your Level Achievements

At levels 2, 5, and 8, you automatically receive:

  • A new Experience at +2 modifier
  • A permanent +1 to your Proficiency (which also increases your weapon damage dice)
  • At levels 5 and 8: clear all marks on character traits you've previously increased

Step 2 — Choose Two Advancements

Pick two from the list below (Tier 3+ adds more options). Each option can be chosen up to 3 times across a tier unless noted:

  • +1 bonus to two unmarked character traits (mark them — can't increase again until the next tier)
  • Permanently gain one Hit Point slot
  • Permanently gain one Stress slot
  • +1 bonus to two Experiences
  • Take an additional domain card (your level or lower)
  • +1 to your Evasion

Tier 3 Additional Options (Levels 5–7)

  • Upgrade your subclass card — take the next tier (Foundation → Specialization → Mastery). Costs one advancement slot.
  • Increase Proficiency by +1 — costs both advancement slots for the level.
  • Multiclass — choose a second class, gain access to one of its domains and its class feature. Costs both advancement slots, available once.

Step 3 — Raise Damage Thresholds

After choosing advancements, your damage thresholds go up by +1 (your thresholds always equal your base + current level). Update your Major and Severe threshold values.

Step 4 — Take a Domain Card

Take one new domain card at your level or lower from a domain your class has access to. If you're over 5 cards total, decide what goes in your vault. You may also trade one current card for a different one of equal or lower level.

Experiences

Experiences represent things your character knows from their past — skills, knowledge, or expertise that don't fit neatly into the six traits. You start with two, gain one at each level achievement, and can increase their modifier over time.

Using an Experience

  • Spend 1 Hope to add your Experience modifier to an action or reaction roll where it applies
  • Wizards (School of Knowledge) can mark a Stress instead of spending Hope, and double their Experience modifier for that roll
  • Experiences are defined by you — they should reflect your character's history, not just game mechanics
Class
Warrior
Melee Fighter · Blade & Bone

Warriors are built for the front line — trained fighters who turn danger into fuel. The more dire the situation, the more dangerous a Warrior becomes. They excel at sustained damage and wearing down whatever stands in front of them.

Hope Feature
No Mercy

Spend 3 Hope to gain a +1 bonus to your attack rolls until your next rest.

Subclasses
Call of the Brave
Use the might of your enemies to fuel your own power.
Courage (Foundation)

When you fail a roll with Fear, you gain a Hope.

Battle Ritual (Foundation)

Once per long rest, before you attempt something incredibly dangerous or face a foe who clearly outmatches you, describe your ritual or preparations. Clear 2 Stress and gain 2 Hope.

Rise to the Challenge (Specialization)

While you have 2 or fewer Hit Points remaining, you can roll a d20 as your Hope Die.

Camaraderie (Mastery)

You can initiate a Tag Team Roll one additional time per session. When an ally initiates a Tag Team Roll with you, they only need to spend 2 Hope.

Call of the Slayer
Strike down adversaries with immense force.
Slayer (Foundation)

Gain a pool of Slayer Dice. On a roll with Hope, place a d6 in the pool instead of taking Hope. Pool maximum equals your Proficiency. Spend Slayer Dice on attack or damage rolls. At session end, clear unspent dice and gain 1 Hope per die cleared.

Weapon Specialist (Specialization)

When you succeed on an attack, spend a Hope to add damage dice from a secondary weapon. Once per long rest, reroll any 1s on your Slayer Dice.

Martial Preparation (Mastery)

Your party gains the Martial Preparation downtime move. During a rest, describe training with your group — you and each ally who chooses this move gain a d6 Slayer Die.

Class
Ranger
Hunter & Tracker · Bone & Sage

Rangers are patient, precise, and dangerous at range. They can lock onto a target and pursue with relentless focus — and they know the wilderness better than most people know their own homes.

Hope Feature
Hold Them Off

Spend 3 Hope when you succeed on an attack to use that same roll against two additional adversaries within range.

Class Feature
Ranger's Focus

Spend a Hope and make an attack. On a success, deal normal damage and temporarily mark the target as your Focus. While they are your Focus, you gain bonuses against them.

Subclasses
Beastbound
Form a deep bond with an animal ally.
Companion (Foundation)

You have an animal companion of your choice (at GM's discretion). They stay by your side unless told otherwise. When you level up, you also choose a level-up option for your companion.

Expert Training + Battle-Bonded (Specialization)

Choose an additional level-up option for your companion. When an adversary attacks you while within your companion's Melee range, gain a +2 bonus to your Evasion against that attack.

Advanced Training + Loyal Friend (Mastery)

Choose two additional level-up options for your companion. Once per long rest, when damage would mark your companion's last Stress or your last HP, you can intervene for each other.

Wayfinder
Hunt your prey and strike with deadly force.
Ruthless Predator (Foundation)

When you make a damage roll, mark a Stress to gain +1 to your Proficiency. When you deal Severe damage, the target must mark a Stress.

Path Forward (Foundation)

When traveling to a previously visited location or using an object that has been there, you can identify the shortest, most direct path.

Elusive Predator (Specialization)

When your Focus makes an attack against you, you gain benefits to evade and counter.

Class
Rogue
Trickster & Operative · Midnight & Grace

Rogues are fast, clever, and built for situations most people don't survive. They exploit openings, disappear when needed, and always seem to know someone useful in the next town.

Hope Feature
Rogue's Dodge

Spend 3 Hope to gain a +2 bonus to your Evasion until the next time an attack succeeds against you.

Subclasses
Nightwalker
Manipulate shadows to move through the environment.
Shadow Stepper (Foundation)

Move from shadow to shadow. When you move into darkness or a shadow cast by another creature or object, mark a Stress to disappear and reappear in another shadow within Far range. When you reappear, you are Cloaked.

Dark Cloud (Specialization)

Make a Spellcast Roll (15). On success, create a temporary dark cloud covering any area within Close range — no one can see through it in either direction. You're Cloaked from adversaries the cloud blocks.

Adrenaline (Specialization)

While you're Vulnerable, add your level to your damage rolls.

Syndicate
Maintain a web of contacts everywhere you go.
Well-Connected (Foundation)

When you arrive in a prominent location, you know somebody there. Name them, note how they're useful, and choose one fact: they owe you a favor (but are hard to find), they'll want something in return, they're always in trouble, you used to be together, or you didn't part well.

Contacts Everywhere (Specialization)

Once per session, call on a shady contact. Choose one: they provide gold or a needed item; give +3 to your next Hope or Fear Die result; or add 2d8 damage to your next attack.

Reliable Backup (Mastery)

You can use Contacts Everywhere more frequently and with greater effect.

Class
Guardian
Protector & Tank · Bone & Valor

Guardians absorb punishment and keep fighting. They protect their allies by standing between them and whatever wants to kill them — and have an uncanny ability to get angrier the more they're hit.

Hope Feature
Frontline Tank

Spend 3 Hope to clear 2 Armor Slots.

Class Feature
Unstoppable

Once per long rest, become Unstoppable. Gain an Unstoppable Die (d4 at level 1, d6 at level 5). Start at value 1; increase by one each time you deal 1+ HP of damage. When the value would exceed maximum, the mode ends. While Unstoppable: reduce damage severity by one threshold, add the die's current value to damage, and you can't be Restrained or Vulnerable.

Subclasses
Stalwart
Take heavy blows and keep fighting.
Unwavering + Iron Will (Foundation)

Gain a permanent +1 to your damage thresholds. When you take physical damage, mark an additional Armor Slot to reduce severity.

Unrelenting + Partners-in-Arms (Specialization)

Gain a permanent +2 to damage thresholds. When an ally within Very Close range takes damage, mark an Armor Slot to reduce severity by one threshold.

Undaunted + Loyal Protector (Mastery)

Gain a permanent +3 to damage thresholds. When an ally within Close range has 2 or fewer HP and would take damage, mark a Stress to sprint to their side and take the hit instead.

Vengeance
Strike down enemies who harm you or your allies.
At Ease + Revenge (Foundation)

Gain an additional Stress slot. When an adversary within Melee range succeeds on an attack against you, mark 2 Stress to force that attacker to mark a Hit Point.

Act of Reprisal (Specialization)

When an adversary damages an ally within Melee range, gain a +1 to your Proficiency for your next successful attack against that adversary.

Nemesis (Mastery)

Spend 2 Hope to Prioritize an adversary until your next rest. When attacking your Prioritized adversary, you can swap the results of your Hope and Fear Dice. One Prioritized target at a time.

Class
Bard
Performer & Support · Grace & Splendor

Bards lift others up through music, words, or sheer force of personality. They're the glue of a party — buffing allies, distracting enemies, and pulling off feats of social manipulation warriors can only dream of.

Hope Feature

Spend 3 Hope to temporarily Distract a target within Close range, giving them a -2 penalty to their Difficulty.

Subclasses
Troubadour
Play music to bolster your allies.

Perform for others. Each song can be used once per long rest: Relaxing Song — you and all allies within Close range clear a Hit Point. Epic Song — make a target within Close range temporarily Vulnerable. Heartbreaking Song — you and all allies within Close range gain a Hope.

Maestro (Specialization)

When you give a Rally Die to an ally, they can gain a Hope or clear a Stress.

Virtuoso (Mastery)

You can perform each Gifted Performer song twice instead of once per long rest.

Wordsmith
Use wordplay and captivate crowds.
Rousing Speech (Foundation)

Once per long rest, give a heartfelt speech. All allies within Far range clear 2 Stress.

Heart of a Poet (Foundation)

After making an action roll to impress, persuade, or offend someone, spend a Hope to add a d4 to the roll.

Eloquent (Specialization)

Once per session when you encourage an ally, choose one: let them find a needed mundane object; Help an Ally without spending Hope; or give them an additional downtime move during their next rest.

Class
Druid
Nature Mage & Shapeshifter · Arcana & Sage

Druids are tied to the living world — they can channel the raw power of natural elements or transform into creatures of the wild. They're unpredictable in the best way, bending the environment to their needs.

Hope Feature
Evolution

Spend 3 Hope to transform into a Beastform without marking a Stress. When you do, raise one trait by +1 until you drop out of that form.

Subclasses
Warden of the Elements
Embody the natural elements of the wild.
Elemental Incarnation (Foundation)

Mark a Stress to Channel one element until you take Severe damage or your next rest. Fire: adversaries in Melee take 1d10 magic damage when they hit you. Earth: bonus to damage thresholds equal to Proficiency. Water: when you deal Melee damage, other nearby adversaries mark a Stress. Air: hover, advantage on Agility Rolls.

Elemental Aura (Specialization)

Once per rest while Channeling, assume an aura within Close range matching your element — each element provides a persistent battlefield effect for you and nearby creatures.

Elemental Dominion (Mastery)

While Channeling, gain a powerful additional benefit tied to your element — enhanced Proficiency, damage reduction, counterattack opportunities, or flight.

Warden of Renewal
Use powerful magic to heal and restore your party.
Clarity of Nature (Foundation)

Once per long rest, create a space of natural serenity within Close range. After a few minutes of rest inside it, clear Stress equal to your Instinct, distributed between you and your allies as you choose.

Class
Seraph
Divine Warrior · Codex & Splendor

Seraphs serve a higher power — divine warriors who carry prayer like a weapon. Prayer Dice give them flexible support and offense, and their bond with their god shapes everything about how they fight and protect.

Hope Feature
Life Support

Spend 3 Hope to clear a Hit Point on an ally within Close range.

Class Feature
Prayer Dice

At the start of each session, roll a number of d4s equal to your Spellcast trait and place them on your sheet. Spend any number to aid yourself or an ally within Far range: reduce incoming damage, add to a roll result after it's made, or gain Hope equal to the die result. Clear all unspent Prayer Dice at session end. Spellcast trait: Strength.

Subclasses
Divine Protector
Wield a spirit weapon and heal those in your care.
Spirit Weapon (Foundation)

When you have a Melee or Very Close weapon equipped, it can fly from your hand to attack an adversary within Close range and return. Mark a Stress to target an additional adversary within range with the same roll.

Sparing Touch (Foundation)

Once per long rest, touch a creature and clear 2 Hit Points or 2 Stress from them.

Devout (Specialization)

When you roll your Prayer Dice, roll an additional die and discard the lowest. Sparing Touch can be used twice per long rest.

Sacred Resonance (Mastery)

When rolling damage for Spirit Weapon, if any die results match, double the value of each matching die. (Two 5s become two 10s.)

Winged Sentinel
Take flight and strike crushing blows from the sky.
Wings of Light (Foundation)

You can fly. While flying: mark a Stress to pick up and carry a willing creature approximately your size or smaller; spend a Hope to deal an extra 1d8 damage on a successful attack.

Ethereal Visage (Specialization)

While flying, you have advantage on Presence Rolls. When you succeed with Hope on a Presence Roll, remove a Fear from the GM's pool instead of gaining Hope.

Class

Raw Magic Caster · Arcana & Midnight

Sorcerers were born with magic in their blood. They don't learn spells so much as wrestle them into shape. High risk, high reward — volatile and powerful in equal measure.

Hope Feature
Volatile Magic

Spend 3 Hope to reroll any number of damage dice on an attack that deals magic damage.

Subclasses
Elemental Origin
Channel raw magic shaped into a particular element.
Elementalist (Foundation)

Choose one element (air, earth, fire, lightning, water) at character creation. You can shape it into harmless effects. Spend a Hope and describe how your element helps a roll — gain either a +2 bonus to the roll or a +3 to the roll's damage.

Natural Evasion (Specialization)

When an attack roll against you succeeds, mark a Stress and describe how your element defends you. Roll a d6 and add it to your Evasion against that attack.

Transcendence (Mastery)

Once per long rest, transform into a physical manifestation of your element. Choose two benefits until your next rest: +4 to Severe threshold, +1 to a character trait, +1 to Proficiency, or +2 to Evasion.

Primal Origin
Extend and twist the essence of your spells.
Manipulate Magic (Foundation)

After casting a spell or making an attack with a magic weapon, mark a Stress to do one of: extend reach by one range; gain +2 to the roll result; double a damage die of your choice; or hit an additional target within range.

Enchanted Aid (Specialization)

Enhance the magic of others using your Duality Dice results.

Class

Wizards earn their power through study. They carry a deeper library of domain cards than most, and their Experiences let them apply specialized knowledge at critical moments. Prepared, methodical, and formidable.

Subclasses

Ancestry
Clank

Sentient mechanical beings built from metal, wood, stone, and stranger materials. They can resemble humanoids, animals, or inanimate objects. No two Clanks are built the same. Many embrace body modifications for style and function, and other ancestries often turn to Clank artisans for customized aids and adornments. A Clank's lifespan extends as long as they can acquire or craft new parts — effectively immortal in body, but their minds deteriorate as the magic powering them loses potency.

Ancestry Features
Purposeful Design

Decide who made you and for what purpose. At character creation, choose one of your Experiences that best aligns with this purpose and gain a permanent +1 bonus to it.

Efficient

When you take a short rest, you can choose a long rest move instead of a short rest move.

Ancestry
Drakona

Wingless dragons in humanoid form, 5 to 7 feet tall, with thick scales providing excellent natural armor and long sharp teeth. They possess a powerful elemental breath inherited from draconic lineage — an element passed through generations, though it can differ from family in rare cases. Unlike their dragon ancestors, Drakona lack wings and can't fly without magical aid. They live approximately 350 years.

Ancestry Features
Scales

Your scales act as natural protection. When you would take Severe damage, you can mark a Stress to mark 1 fewer Hit Point.

Elemental Breath

Choose an element for your breath (such as electricity, fire, or ice). Use it against a target or group within Very Close range, treating it as an Instinct weapon that deals d8 magic damage using your Proficiency.

Ancestry
Dwarf

Short humanoids (4 to 5½ feet) with square frames, dense musculature, and thick hair. Their skin and nails contain a high amount of keratin, making them naturally resilient — allowing them to embed gemstones or wear elaborate tattoos and piercings. All genders can grow thick facial hair, often styled elaborately. Dwarves live up to 250 years and maintain their muscle mass well into later life.

Ancestry Features
Thick Skin

When you take Minor damage, you can mark 2 Stress instead of marking a Hit Point.

Increased Fortitude

Spend 3 Hope to halve incoming physical damage.

Ancestry
Elf

Tall humanoids (about 6 to 6½ feet) with pointed ears and acutely attuned senses. As they age, the ear tips begin to droop. Elves rest through a celestial trance rather than sleep, recovering effectively in less time. Some develop a "mystic form" from deep dedication to the natural world — celestial freckles, leaves or vines in the hair, eyes that flicker like flame. These traits can shift across their approximately 350-year lifespan.

Ancestry Features
Quick Reactions

Mark a Stress to gain advantage on a reaction roll.

Celestial Trance

During a rest, you can drop into a trance to choose an additional downtime move.

Ancestry
Faerie

Winged humanoids with insectile features ranging from subtle to unmistakable — some with compound eyes, extra arms, chitinous exoskeletons, or stingers. Heights range from 2 to 7 feet. All Faeries have membranous wings and go through a metamorphosis during which they manifest their unique permanent appearance. They live approximately 50 years — short lives, but vivid ones.

Ancestry Features
Luckbender

Once per session, after you or a willing ally within Close range makes an action roll, spend 3 Hope to reroll the Duality Dice.

Wings

You can fly. While flying, mark a Stress after an adversary makes an attack against you to gain a +2 bonus to your Evasion against that attack.

Ancestry
Faun

Humanoid from the torso up, goat-like below — with curving horns, square pupils, and cloven hooves. Height ranges from 4 to 6½ feet but can change dramatically based on stance. Most Fauns have proportionally long limbs and are known for their powerful hooves. They live roughly 225 years, and as they age, their appearance grows increasingly caprine.

Ancestry Features
Caprine Leap

You can leap anywhere within Close range as though using normal movement — vaulting obstacles, jumping gaps, or scaling barriers with ease.

Kick

When you succeed on an attack against a target within Melee range, mark a Stress to kick off them — dealing an extra 2d6 damage and knocking either yourself or the target to Very Close range.

Ancestry
Firbolg

Bovine humanoids (5 to 7 feet) recognized by broad noses and long drooping ears. Some have faces blending human and bison, others ("minotaurs") have fully bovine heads.
